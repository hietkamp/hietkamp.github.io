    Business Scenarios
    as an Essence practice

    The TOGAF Business Scenario technique, re-expressed as a small, composable Essence practice. It adds almost no new structure — instead it becomes the engine that advances alphas you already have, sized the MVA way: one living document, not a wall of matrices.

    TOGAF Business ScenariosEssence practiceMVA-leanCustomer area + front of the ADM loopLevel · Enterprise / capability

    Where it plugs in

    The Customer area and the start of the ADM loop — inside Manage Concerns and Frame the Vision.

    What it adds

    One sub-alpha, six activities, one work product, twelve roles and four patterns — composed onto the kernel Customer spaces.

    What it advances

    Opportunity, Stakeholders, Requirements and Architectural Drivers — it's a way to get Drivers to Quantified.

    MVA discipline

    One Business Scenario document; SMART outcomes become measurable success criteria; stop at fit-for-purpose.

    Show

    This practice is a synthesis: it expresses the Business Scenario method in Essence terms and maps it onto the existing Kernel + MVA/MVG deck. It paraphrases the technique rather than reproducing The Open Group guide — keep your licensed copy of the TOGAF® Series Guide: Business Scenarios as the authoritative source for the method itself. "Drives the alpha" notation shows which alpha state each element pushes toward.
